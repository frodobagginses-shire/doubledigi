About the Double-Digi format & the pitch

The elevator pitch

Double-Digi delivers the excitement and variability of a surprise set — chase spots, runaway pulls, edge-of-your-seat luck — without the parts WhatNot's ToS now suppresses. Surprise sets are getting throttled and downranked; Double-Digi keeps that energy in a format the algorithm is happy to push, which means a bigger audience.

How it works

100 fixed spots, numbered 00 through 99. Every rare or mythic pulled gets sorted by the last two digits of its collector number. Spot 42 collects #42, #142, #242, etc. Commons and uncommons go to a shared Bulk spot. Secret Lair drops and commander precons add guaranteed hits to specific spot numbers based on those cards' own collector numbers.

Why it runs better than Digi / Color

Digi and Color breaks can sit in limbo for ages. The streamer ends up burning cash on give-away packs trying to fill the last 4 spots while the auctions stall. Double-Digi's lower barrier to entry — 100 cheap spots instead of a handful of expensive colors or digits — pulls more people in. Auctions move, spots fill, the break doesn't choke.

And because the spots cluster naturally around hot collector-number ranges (R/M cards aren't uniformly distributed across the set's numbering), the format always has clear chase spots. Hype the 3-star spots, fill the 1-star spots cheap, and ride the variance just like a surprise set.

📖 How EV is Calculated

Pack Simulation

Wherever possible the simulator uses MTGJSON's authoritative booster data — the same sheet-and-variant model WotC uses for actual pack collation. Each booster type is defined by:

Variants: a set of pack templates with different slot compositions, each with a weight. SOS Play Boosters, for example, have 4 variants with weights 216 / 54 / 4 / 1 out of 275 total.

Sheets: weighted card pools that fill each slot — mysticalArchive, specialGuest, foilUMysticalArchive, etc.

To open a pack, the simulator picks one variant by weight, then for each (sheet, count) pair in that variant picks the requested number of cards from the sheet's weighted pool. This captures bonus sheet rates, special-guest rates, etched/silverscroll treatments, and any per-set quirks exactly as Wizards specifies.

For sets without MTGJSON booster data (typically very old sets or unreleased products), a simplified slot model is used as a fallback.

Bonus Sheets & Special Treatments

Bonus sheets are modeled as their own sheets within the variant model. Per-pack rate is determined by variant weights, not a flat percentage.

SOS Mystical Archive: guaranteed slot in every Play Booster (100% per pack).

SOS Silverscroll (Collector Booster): foil R/M Mystical Archive sheet appears in ~73% of CB variants, with silverscrolls weighted at ~13.6% within that sheet — net ~10% of CBs contain a silverscroll.

Special Guest (SPG): typically ~1.3–1.5% per pack, drawn from a 10-card sheet.

OTJ Breaking News: ~19% per Play Booster from MTGJSON's combined "the list" sheet.

EV Calculation

For every R/M card C that can appear in a pack of the given booster type:

P(C per pack) = Σ over variants v: P(v) × Σ over sheets s in v: counts × weight(C in s) / totalWeight(s)

Then EV(C) = price(C) × P(C per pack) × pack count, summed across the entire roster.

The Spot EV for spot NN is the sum of EV contributions from every R/M card whose collector number ends in NN — e.g. Spot 42 collects #042, #142, #242, etc.

Secret Lair Drops

Secret Lair Drops are deterministic. Their EV contribution is Σ (card price × quantity) with no probability factor. Finishes (regular / foil / rainbow foil) use Scryfall's prices.usd, prices.usd_foil, and prices.usd_etched respectively. Each card lands in the spot indicated by its collector number within the master sld set.

Commander Precons

Commander precons are also deterministic. The simulator pulls full decklists from MTGJSON when available (typically 100-card decks with an isFoil flag on the face card), falling back to "face cards only" for unreleased products. Each card lands in the spot indicated by its own collector number within its printing set.

Important Notes

Prices come from TCGplayer Market via Scryfall. Only R/M cards contribute to spot EV; commons and uncommons go to Bulk regardless of collector number. Sheet weights from MTGJSON capture set-specific rarities like surge foils and silverscrolls; serialized cards and very-low-probability headliner slots (<0.5%) are not always modeled.

📦 Break Roster

What is the Break Roster?

The Break Roster is your product pool — every booster pack and Secret Lair Drop being opened in the break. Use the toggle at the top to switch between adding Booster Packs and Secret Lair Drops. Card data is fetched from Scryfall when you add a row, so pricing and EV calculations are accurate.

Booster Pack Types

Play Booster — The standard retail booster sold since early 2024. Contains commons, uncommons, one guaranteed R/M slot, two wildcard slots, one guaranteed foil, and a land. Sets released before Feb 2024 used Draft or Set Boosters instead.

Collector Booster — Premium booster containing foil, extended-art, borderless, and surge-foil versions of cards. Higher EV per pack but significantly higher cost. The tool models treatment-specific slots.

Draft / Set Booster — Pre-2024 formats. Both are supported for older sets.

Secret Lair Drops

Each Secret Lair Drop is a fixed envelope of cards sold direct from Wizards. Type the drop name to search; choose foil or non-foil if both finishes exist. Contents are deterministic, so every drop card lands in the spot indicated by its collector number within the master sld set.

Subset / Bonus Sheet Cards

Some sets include a bonus card from a sub-set (e.g. TMNT Pizza Sheet, OTJ Breaking News). When you add a set with a known bonus sheet, the tool automatically fetches both the main set and the subset for accurate EV. Subset cards use their own collector numbering for spot assignment.

Tips

Mix products freely — a Double-Digi break can combine boosters across multiple sets and Secret Lair envelopes in the same roster. The summary at the bottom shows total products and estimated card count.

🎯 What is a Double-Digi Break?

The Concept

A Double-Digi Break is a group buy format with 100 fixed numeric spots, numbered 00 through 99. Each opened rare or mythic card is assigned to a spot based on the last two digits of its collector number — no tribe or type matching, no priority order, just simple math.

How Spots Work

Card #042 lands in Spot 42. Card #137 lands in Spot 37. Card #100, #200, #300 all land in Spot 00. Suffixes like ★ or f are stripped (so 230★ still goes to Spot 30). Subset cards (Special Guests, OTJ Big Score, etc.) use their own collector numbering inside their subset, not the host set's.

All commons and uncommons go to Bulk, regardless of collector number. R/M cards with no parseable digits (extremely rare) fall into a residual Last Spot.

Why Double-Digi

Trivially fair. No debate about which tribe a card belongs to or who picks first.

Roster-dependent variance. Within any given set, R/M cards cluster in specific number ranges — so some spots are statistically much hotter than others. The Spot Distribution Analysis heatmap shows you which spots are valuable for your specific roster before you commit.

Secret Lair friendly. Each Secret Lair Drop has its own card-number range within the master sld set, so envelope contents land deterministically into specific numeric spots.

🎬 Break Builder — How It Works

What this tab is for

Break Builder is the streamer side of the calculator. Buyer Mode answers "what's a spot worth?" Break Builder answers "how do I price spots, name them, and run the auction?"

Cost basis

The Roster table in this tab grows a Cost/Unit column. Enter your purchase price per pack, per Secret Lair envelope, or per commander deck. The Cost Summary card shows your total roster cost and a break-even per spot (cost ÷ 100). That break-even is the floor your spot prices need to clear — before you add givvies, employee costs, or your profit margin.

Star tiers

Spots are ranked by expected value. The top N go to ⭐⭐⭐, next N to ⭐⭐, next N to ⭐, and the rest plain. You can tune N with the Star Tier Settings card. Default thresholds: top 10 / next 20 / next 30.

If a 3-star spot has a guaranteed hit (SLD card or precon card landing in that spot) priced at or above the notable threshold, the label gets a callout: ⭐⭐⭐01 Demonic Tutor Guaranteed Hit ⭐⭐⭐.

Spot Labels table

One row per spot. Columns:

Label — the streamer-facing label to paste into your overlay.

EV — expected dollar value of the spot for this roster.

Guaranteed Hits — cards locked into the spot from SLDs and precons (with quantity and price).

Top Potential Hits — probabilistic rares/mythics from boosters priced at or above the notable threshold (default $20).

The 📋 button on each row copies that one label. The Copy All Labels button at the top of the table copies all 100 newline-separated.
